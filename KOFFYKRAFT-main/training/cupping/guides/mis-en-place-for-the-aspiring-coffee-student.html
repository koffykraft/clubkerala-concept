Beginner guide

Mis en Place for the Aspiring Coffee Student

How to use this: Read once before a session, then return only to the checklist you need. The goal is calmer practice, not more pressure.

Before beginning your journey through coffee cupping, sensory learning, and brewing, it's essential to prepare both yourself and your space. This 'mis en place' document is your checklist - to calm your mind, set your table, and create an environment where learning feels possible, joyful, and repeatable.

  • Choose a clean, quiet space where you feel safe and focused.
  • Natural light is ideal. If not possible, use warm white LED lighting.
  • Use a small table or counter with enough room for cups, spoons, and your notes.
  • Avoid strong smells nearby - incense, perfumes, cooking - as they distort aroma detection.
  • Sit comfortably. If standing, ensure your back and shoulders are relaxed.
  • Ideal room temperature: 22-25 deg C. Slightly cooler is fine.
  • Humidity should be moderate (40-60%). Use a fan or window for ventilation.
  • Sound: calm background or silence preferred. Music okay if soft and familiar.
  • Coffee grinder (manual or electric)
  • Digital scale (0.1g accuracy preferred)
  • Kettle (preferably gooseneck, but any will do)
  • Timer or stopwatch (phone is fine)
  • Spoon (preferably cupping spoon or deep soup spoon)
  • Cups (150-200ml, ideally with round bowl shape)
  • Notebook or printed cupping log
  • Optional: TDS meter, thermometer, flavor wheel printout
  • Start with 2-3 coffees of different origin or roast.
  • Use clean, odorless water. Filtered or boiled-and-cooled tap water works well.
  • Ideal brewing water temperature: 92-96 deg C.
  • Avoid spicy, heavily seasoned food or strong drinks before cupping.
  • Rinse mouth with clean water. Avoid toothpaste within 1 hour of cupping.
  • Take 3 deep breaths before starting. Focus your mind on the coffee, not the result.
  • Remind yourself: You are here to learn, not perform. There is no 'wrong' note.
  • Curiosity > Confidence. Stay open to surprise.
  • Describe first, judge later.
  • Use your own words - emotional, visual, even strange - to describe sensations.
  • Sensory memory builds over time. Today's notes will help tomorrow's clarity.

Carry this into your next cupping

  • Prepare the space before preparing the coffee.
  • Use fewer cups if you feel overloaded.
  • Write what you notice before checking what others say.
  • Repeat one exercise twice before judging progress.
