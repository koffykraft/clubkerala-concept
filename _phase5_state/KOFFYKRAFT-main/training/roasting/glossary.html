Roasting Glossary

Short definitions used across the course.

Charge temperature

The machine reading at green coffee loading. Interpret it by machine, batch size, probe placement, and preheat routine.

Turning point

The low point on many roast curves after charge. Useful for consistency, but not a flavor goal by itself.

Dry end

A practical marker where green/grassy character gives way toward yellowing and bread-like aromas.

Maillard phase

Browning reactions involving sugars and amino compounds. Important for aroma, color, sweetness, and complexity.

First Crack

Audible structural event used as a practical control marker. It does not mean all browning chemistry has stopped.

Development

The practical final phase after First Crack where roast depth, sweetness, acidity, body, and balance are shaped.

Rate of Rise

The speed of temperature increase. Treat it as a diagnostic signal, not a moral score.

DTR

Development Time Ratio. Useful for comparing roasts, not sufficient for defining roast quality.

Solubility

How readily roasted coffee dissolves into brewing water. Roasting affects it, but grind, water, and method also matter.

Baked

A dull, flat, or lifeless cup impression often associated with poor momentum or excessive time, but it must be confirmed by cup and log.
