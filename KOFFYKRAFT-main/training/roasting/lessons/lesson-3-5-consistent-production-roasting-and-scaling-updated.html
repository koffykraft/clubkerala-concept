Consistent Production Roasting & Scaling (Updated)

The roasting learning loop

  1. Intend: name the coffee, brew use, and flavor goal before heating the machine.
  2. Plan: choose batch size, charge approach, airflow, milestones, and one variable to watch.
  3. Roast and log: record time, temperature, colour, aroma, sound, airflow, gas or power, and decisions.
  4. Cup: evaluate with the Cupping course discipline before defending the profile.
  5. Brew: test with a realistic brew method from the Brewing course.
  6. Revise: change one thing in the next roast and write why.
What this lesson teaches

Production scaling means preserving cup quality while batch size, machine load, workflow, and risk increase.

How this builds the student

This lesson builds operational discipline.

Cupping connection

Use aroma, sweetness, acidity, body, aftertaste, balance, defects, and confidence notes from the Cupping course. A roast decision is not proven until the cup supports it.

Brewing connection

Check whether the roast extracts sensibly in a real recipe. Sour, hollow, bitter, harsh, or muddy brews may point back to roast development, solubility, or brew setup.

From the KoffyKraft notes

Learning Goal

Master consistent roasting practices for batch-to-batch and scaled-up production, integrating checkpoints, tools, and adjustments that preserve origin flavor and QC integrity.

A. Why Consistency Matters

  • Predictable flavor builds customer loyalty- Reduces re-roasting and waste- Protects origin identity through scaling- Empowers feedback from baristas and brewers

B. Batch-to-Batch Consistency Factors

FactorControl Strategy
Charge TempSame warm-up routine + target charge temp
Green ConditionUse same lot; store cool, dry, and sealed
Roast CurveMatch RoR, crack timing, development %
EnvironmentLog humidity/temp; adjust airflow or charge
Weighing AccuracyCalibrate scales, timers, thermometers weekly

C. Scaling Up Roast Size (with Origin Tips)

Scaling ElementTip for SuccessOrigin Notes
Drum Size IncreaseTest drum heat retention and batch controlYirgacheffe -> longer dev for density
Airflow VolumeAdjust fan/gas settings to match profile shapeRobusta -> tweak airflow to avoid scorching
Bean DepthStir or rotate for even heat exposureLarger batches -> more internal thermal variation
Development TargetKeep DTR% same, extend time by 10-15 sec if neededYirgacheffe -> maintain sweetness with longer dev
Sweetness LossCheck airflow, check tipping, check RoR plateauLower density -> easier to overshoot

F. Analogy - Like Playing in a Symphony

  • A roaster in production is like a percussionist: steady, precise, no improvisation- Every roast is a repeat performance of a tested score- Practice delivers predictability, not perfection on accident

H. Sustainability Note

  • Fewer failed batches = less fuel + fewer defects- Helps honor origin clarity - e.g., Yirgacheffe florals- Fairer use of farmer effort and crop potential- Consistency = less waste, more traceable value

I. Sample Reflection Prompt

Prompt: "I scaled from 1kg to 5kg. Body held but citrus dropped."Answer: Likely heat transfer imbalance. Check airflow and charge. Extend Maillard slightly, reduce early gas. Keep dev smooth - not rushed.

Practice assignment

  1. State the roast intention in one sentence.
  2. Roast, observe, or study a roast video with the lesson idea in mind.
  3. Cup the result or a comparable coffee using a simple written form.
  4. Brew the same coffee with one normal method and compare it to the cupping result.
  5. Write one next-roast change and one reason not to change anything else.

Common beginner mistakes

  • Trusting the curve before tasting the coffee.
  • Changing charge, airflow, batch size, and development all at once.
  • Using one roast defect word as a verdict without checking green coffee, machine, log, and cup.
  • Ignoring brew behavior after a roast cups well.

Self-check with answer guide

1. What should this lesson change in your roasting judgment?

Answer: It should give you one clearer observation or decision rule, not a memorized number.

2. How do cupping and brewing protect you from false confidence?

Answer: Cupping isolates roast and green coffee signals; brewing tests whether the roast works in the way someone will actually drink it.

3. What is the safest next experiment?

Answer: Repeat the roast with one planned change, keep the same evaluation method, and compare notes side by side.
